Stevens History

Founders

Edwin A. Stevens died in 1868. In his will, he left a request for the establishment of an "institution of learning," providing his trustees with land and funds. Edwinâ€™s will was executed by surviving wife Martha Stevens who would also serve as a lifetime Trustee of the Institute.

Establishing the University

Stevens Institute of Technology opened in 1870. The campus began on the edge of the family estate at Castle Point in Hoboken. The honor code was established in 1906, and was the first of its kind at a technical school.

Recent History

Stevens' undergraduate program became coed in 1971. In 1982, Stevens was the first institution in the U.S. to require all incoming freshman undergraduate students to purchase and use a personal computer. Currently Stevens enrolls 3,123 undergraduate students.
